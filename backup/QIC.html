Quick Information Chart (Christian Celebrations)

 

Celebration Date/Occurrence Key Practices Significance
The Lord's Day Every Sunday Scripture readings, prayers, hymns, Eucharist, offerings Commemorates Jesus' resurrection; replaces the Jewish Sabbath
Pascha (Easter) 20 April 2025, 5 April 2026 Fasting, catechesis, vigils, scripture readings, sunrise Eucharist Celebrates Christ's resurrection; symbolizes new creation and hope
Pentecost 1 June 2025, 21 May 2026 Communal prayer, scripture readings, Eucharist, baptisms Marks the descent of the Holy Spirit; beginning of the Church's mission
Martyrs' Commemorations Annually (death date of the martyr) Prayers, Eucharist, shared meals, storytelling Honors martyrs' sacrifice; inspires faith and courage
Agape Feasts No fixed date Communal meals, sharing resources, acts of charity Emphasizes unity, charity, and Christ's love
Baptism No fixed date Fasting, prayers, confession of faith, laying on of hands Symbolizes repentance, forgiveness, and new life in Christ
Fasting Wednesdays and Fridays Abstinence from food, prayer, spiritual reflection Prepares for major feasts; fosters humility and penance
Catechesis and Instruction Pre-baptismal Teaching, moral guidance, confession, ethical practices Prepares converts for baptism and Christian living
Ancillary Activities As needed Teaching, fellowship, charity, communal sharing Extends faith beyond formal worship; builds community
