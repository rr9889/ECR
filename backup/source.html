Early Christian Writings (Pre-325 AD)

By Ryan Reeves

In the year of our Lord 2025, on the third day of January...

Knowledge Transfer Article (KTA)

Introduction

This list includes every known work by Church Fathers pre-325 AD, organized by author and date. It covers major writings, fragments, and anonymous texts that provide insight into early Christian theology, worship, and practice.

1st Century

The New Testament

Various authors, 1st century

The foundational texts of Christianity, including the Gospels, Acts, Epistles, and Revelation.

Category: Scripture

Late 1st–Early 2nd Century

The Didache

Anonymous, late 1st–early 2nd century

A manual of Christian ethics, worship, and church order, often referred to as "The Teaching of the Twelve Apostles."

Category: Church Manual

The Epistle of Barnabas

Anonymous, early 2nd century

A theological treatise emphasizing the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies in Christ.

Category: Theology

First Epistle of Clement

Clement of Rome, c. 96 AD

A letter addressing church order and unity, written to the Corinthian church.

Category: Epistle

Early 2nd Century

Pliny the Younger (Epistles 10.96)

Pliny the Younger, c. 112 AD

A Roman governor’s letter describing early Christian worship practices, including hymn-singing and communal meals.

Category: Historical Account

Letters of Ignatius of Antioch

Ignatius of Antioch, c. 107–110 AD

Seven letters written to various churches, emphasizing unity, the Eucharist, and the authority of bishops.

Category: Epistles

Mid-2nd Century

First Apology

Justin Martyr, c. 150–155 AD

A defense of Christianity addressed to the Roman emperor, explaining Christian beliefs and practices.

Category: Apologetics

Late 2nd Century

Against Heresies (Adversus Haereses)

Irenaeus of Lyons, late 2nd century

A comprehensive refutation of Gnosticism, defending orthodox Christian teachings.

Category: Theology

Late 2nd–Early 3rd Century

Apology

Tertullian, late 2nd–early 3rd century

A defense of Christianity against Roman accusations, emphasizing the rationality and morality of Christian beliefs.

Category: Apologetics

Early 3rd Century

On First Principles (De Principiis)

Origen, early 3rd century

A foundational work of Christian theology, exploring the nature of God, creation, and salvation.

Category: Theology

Apostolic Tradition

Hippolytus of Rome, early 3rd century

A liturgical manual detailing early Christian worship practices, including baptism and the Eucharist.

Category: Liturgy

Conclusion

This list provides a comprehensive overview of early Christian writings, showcasing the rich theological and liturgical traditions of the Church Fathers before 325 AD.
