Name

The early followers of Jesus, known as the First Christians, adopted several titles reflecting their faith and dedication to His teachings.

Believers

Brothers and Sisters

This term highlights unity, love, and mutual support within the Christian community.

Christian

The term "Christian" (Χρῑστῐᾱνός, Khrīstiānós), derived from the Greek Χριστός (Christos), meaning "the Anointed One," was first used in Acts 11:26 to describe followers of Christ. Likely coined by outsiders in Antioch, it initially served as a label for those associated with Jesus and His teachings. Over time, believers embraced "Christian" as their identity, and it became the widely recognized name for adherents of Christianity.

Disciples

Disciples, derived from the Greek μαθητής (mathētēs) meaning "learner," were early followers of Jesus, committed to understanding His teachings and emulating His life. Initially the Twelve Apostles, the term grew to include all believers devoted to spreading the Gospel through love, humility, and service. Discipleship required full devotion, personal sacrifice, and a life of worship and ethical living (e.g., Matthew 28:19, Acts 6:1).

Saints

"Saints" signifies those set apart as holy for God, sanctified through faith in Christ (e.g., Romans 1:7, 1 Corinthians 1:2). In the Old Testament, this identity stems from obedience and sacrificial worship; in the New Testament, it includes all believers in Christ, justified by faith and called to holiness. Saints live out their calling through worship, prayer, and love.

The Nazarenes

Another title was "The Nazarenes," linked to Jesus of Nazareth, as mentioned in Acts 24:5, where His followers were called "the sect of the Nazarenes." This name pointed to Jesus's origins in Nazareth and fulfilled the prophecy of Him being "called a Nazarene" (Matthew 2:23). It identified His followers with their leader and emphasized His importance in their faith.

The Way

Refers to the distinct path of faith and practice centered on Jesus's teachings and His role as the Messiah. "The Way" (τῆς ὁδοῦ) is mentioned in Acts 9:2, where Saul sought to arrest those who followed this movement. The term emphasizes a transformative way of life and belief rooted in Jesus's message and mission.
