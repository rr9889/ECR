Infant Baptism (Pre-325 AD)

Baptism Requirements

Full Article on Baptism

Summary

  1. Repentance and Faith: Baptism is tied to repentance and faith (Acts 2:38). For infants, proxy faith (parents/Church) fulfills this requirement.
  2. Trinitarian Formula: Baptism is performed in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit (Matthew 28:19).
  3. Use of Water: Baptism requires water, preferably "living water" (running water), though other sources are acceptable (Didache 7:1-2).
  4. Fasting: Both the baptizer and candidate fast before baptism (Didache 7:4). For infants, sponsors or parents fast on their behalf.
  5. Undressing: Symbolizes shedding the "old self" and rising to new life in Christ (Colossians 3:9-10). Nakedness mirrors Christâ€™s death and resurrection (Romans 6:3-4). Tertullian (On Baptism, Chapter 20) and Hippolytus of Rome (Apostolic Tradition) describe this practice, with modesty maintained through separate baptisms for men and women.
  6. Ages: Infants are included as part of the covenant community, reflecting the belief that baptism is for all believers, regardless of age. Origen (Commentary on Romans, Book V, 9) and Cyprian of Carthage (Letters, 64:2) affirm infant baptism as an apostolic tradition, noting its necessity for the remission of original sin.
  7. Symbolism: Baptism symbolizes dying to sin and rising to new life in Christ (Romans 6:4), essential for spiritual rebirth (John 3:5).
