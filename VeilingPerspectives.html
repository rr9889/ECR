Veiling Perspectives: Views on 1 Corinthians 11:2-16

No Applicability View

Argument

This view holds that 1 Corinthians 11:2-16 has no relevance today because Paul discusses a 'tradition' (v. 2) and 'custom' (v. 16) not practiced in modern churches, suggesting it’s optional.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org; Stam, C. R. (1988). Commentary on the First Epistle to the Corinthians.
Critique

Wallace dismisses this view, arguing that 'traditions' (παραδόσεις) in v. 2 and 'custom' (συνήθειαν) in v. 16 are not optional habits but binding doctrines, as seen in Paul’s use of παραδίδωμι elsewhere (e.g., 1 Cor 15:3).

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org.

Head Covering = Hair View

Argument

This view posits that the head covering is a woman’s long hair, based on v. 15 ('her hair is given as a covering'), suggesting women today should maintain long hair as the applicability.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org; Hurley, J. B. (1981). Man and Woman in Biblical Perspective.
Critique

Wallace critiques this view, noting that if hair were the covering, men should shave their heads (v. 7), and v. 6 becomes tautological. Vv. 10 and 15 contrast submission and glory, not equating hair with a veil. The Greek ἀκατάκαλυπτος means 'uncovered,' not 'let loose,' per BAGD.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org; Payne, P. B. (2006). Wild Hair and Gender Equality in 1 Corinthians 11:2–16.

Real Head Covering, Applicable Today View

Arguments

This view asserts a real head covering is in view, applicable today, based on theological foundations (vv. 2-9: creation order; v. 10: angels; vv. 13-15: nature; v. 16: church practice). Sub-views include: (1) all women in church services, (2) only when praying/prophesying publicly.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org; Sproul, R.C. (1977). Knowing Scripture; Lipsy, D. J. (2005). The Headcovering in Worship.
Critique

Wallace finds this exegetically strong but practically challenging today. He prefers the sub-view of covering only during public prayer/prophecy (vv. 4-5, 13), but argues its modern humiliation contradicts Paul’s intent of submission, not shame.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org.

Meaningful Symbol View

Arguments

Wallace’s preferred view: a real head covering was used, but its applicability today requires a culturally relevant symbol of submission, not necessarily a veil. Sub-views mirror the prior view, with Wallace favoring public prayer/prophecy only.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org; Schreiner, T. R. (2005). Head Coverings, Prophecies, and the Trinity.
Justification & Symbol

Justified by: (1) aligning with the spirit (submission) over letter (veil), (2) NT focus on reality over ritual, (3) cultural adaptability (e.g., Lord’s Supper variations). Wallace suggests a modest dress as a modern symbol, reflecting submission without humiliation.

Wallace, D. B. (2004). What is the Head Covering in 1 Cor 11:2-16 and Does it Apply to Us Today? Bible.org; Constable, T. L. (2025). Notes on 1 Corinthians.
